Resources

Literature for the Science Classroom

Chemistry
Chemistry As Fun And Games (pdf)
Interactive games for students to demonstrate they understand introductory chemistry topics like mole, stoichiometry, electron configuration and the periodic table. Excellent ideas for alternative assessment.

The ChemCollective Activities
Simulations, games and problems on the topics of Molarity and Density, Stoichiometry and Limiting Reagents, Quantitative Analysis, Chemical Equilibrium, Solubility and Salts, Thermochemistry and Acids and Bases. An activity of note is a murder mystery called “Mixed Reception” where students must use molecular weight and stoichiometry to solve the case.

List of Previous JCE Classroom Activities
Journal of Chemical Education's list of hands-on activities that can be done in the classroom or at home. Some activities are interdisciplinary.

Chemical Quantities
Activities with worksheets for chemical quantity concepts like molar mass, stoichiometry and balanced chemical equations. The website also includes links to many online chemistry-related games.

The High School Laboratory
Exhaustive list of chemistry activities and demonstrations as well as resources on lab safety and techniques. Currently the Pre-Packaged Lab Kit Review page does not view properly (although I'd imagine when it does work it would be very useful).

Interactive Chemistry
A review site for online chemistry resources that includes learning accessibility level and a “3 star” rating system on playfulness, interactivity and explanation. One draw back to the site is I noticed a couple dead links.

Physics
Mr Milligan's AP Physics
This teacher's advanced placement class webpage includes activities, problems, practice tests and graphing calculator programs. Mechanics topics include kinematics, dynamics, work, energy, linear momentum, rotation, equilibrium, oscillations. Electricity and Magnetism topics include electrostatics, flux, electrical potential, current and circuits, magnetostatics, inductance and capacitance.

Sask Learning Physics 30
Resources supporting the Saskatchewan Physics 30 curricula intended to help correspondence course students. Each section includes lessons, problems, online activities, classroom activities as well as quizzes with solutions.

Physics First with Mr Noon
Physics class website that includes activities, assignments, Powerpoint notes, quizzes and videos. Topics include kinematics, dynamics, momentum, energy, astrophysics, sound, waves, optics, electricity, magnetism and Modern Physics. The site is organized by day so it is interesting to see how an experienced teacher paces his course.

The Ohio Resource Center for Mathematics, Science and Technology Common Student Misconception
A list of student misconceptions of physical properties. Most topics include a lesson and an activity for better student understanding. Topics covered include Forces and Motion, Universal Gravitation, Waves, Nature of Matter and Energy.

The Physics Classroom

Kinematics
Physics First Kinematics: The Physics of Motion Units
This site includes Activities, References and Collections, Content Support For Teachers like lesson plans, Student Tutorials on kinematics topics such as Frame of Reference, Graphing, Vectors, One Dimensional Motion, Motion in More than One Dimension, Circular Motion, Planetary Motion and Velocity and Acceleration.

Physics Source Alberta Physics 20: Kinematics
Kinematic resources that correspond to the Pearson Physics 20 textbook for Alberta. It isn't necessary to have the textbook to be able to use the resources. It includes links to lessons, quizzes, activities, videos and online simulations.

The Humanized Physics Project: Biomechanics and Human Motion
Using human movement as the basis for learning physical concepts such as kinematics and dynamics. Activities are an excellent to get your students moving while they learn. Activities are available in Word format but require knowledge on how to a use a spreadsheet.

Common Student Difficulties with Velocity
A list of common misconceptions students have with speed, velocity and acceleration. Each topic has a description, a discovery activity for students to dispel the misconception and references to the topic.

Electricity & Magnetism
Energy Kids, U.S. Energy Information Administration
The site is geared towards elementary, middle years and secondary students but also an excellent resource for teachers. Sections include

  • What is Energy?
  • Energy Sources
  • Using & Saving Energy
  • History of Energy
  • Games & Activities

A couple areas of note:
The Games & Activities has a Science Fair Experiment section with suitable activities for K-12.
The History of Energy includes contributions of scientists that allowed us to be able to use electricity today. It is written in such a way that it make it easy for student research.
The Teacher Section has an Energy Calculator where you can compare how much electricity is produced by different sources.

Magnet Man: Cool Experiments with Magnets, Rick Hoadley
Many demonstrations and experiments on magnetism properties, interactions with conductors, electromagnets, magnetic levitation, motors and electronic. Some of the activities are on the site but unfortunately some of the activity links to other sites are dead.

The Science of Non-Destructive Testing: High School Education Resource , NDT Resource Center
Many useful explanations and animations for understanding electricity and magnetism concepts. At the end of each section, it includes how the NDT industry uses the discussed properties for research. Sections for the Resource Center include electricity, magnetism, x-rays and sound.

Energy Quest, California Energy Commission
The navigation is little bit of a pain but there are many useful resources on electricity and energy production. Some sections include

  • In the How Does it Work? section answers How Does a Transformer Work?
  • Fuel sources
  • The Story of Energy - what energy is and the many ways it is produced
  • A lot of demonstrations and experiments in the Science Fair Projects section
  • The Movie Room has animations and documentaries on concepts and the history of energy production


Physics GCSE: Magnetism and Electromagnetism, S-Cool
Free standardized text review site for students includes many useful animations and interactive quizzes. The site includes many explanations of applications of magnetism like motors, speakers and transformers

Molecular Expressions: Electricity and Magnetism
Interactive java tutorials to visualize electrical and magnetic concepts and applications. Some tutorials of interest: charging and discharging a capacitor, Ohm's law, building a transistor and how a metal detector works.

Energy and Change Teacher's Guide, Australian Government Department of Education, Employment and Workplace Relations
Units on electricity, energy and light set up using the 5 Es (engage, explore, explain, elaborate, evaluate). Activities include sample questions to ask students to guide their understanding (without having to lecture). The site includes professional development resources on pedagogy and assessment.

Key Stage 3: Physics, skool.co.uk
Narrated animations and simulations on

  • series and parallel circuits
  • circuit diagrams
  • electric current
  • measuring current
  • measuring voltage
  • fuses and circuit breakers
  • understanding magnets
  • magnetism and electric currents
  • plotting a magnetic field
The end of each lesson has a short quiz and review.

BBC Learning Zone Broadband Clips: Electricity
BBC Learning Zone Broadband Clips: Magnets
BBC Learning Zone Broadband Clips: Magnetism
Free documentaries and educational programing on electromagnetism concepts that were originally broadcasted on the BBC.

Nova: Magnetic Storm
Teaching resources are intended to be used with PBS' Nova: Magnetic Storm but most can be used without viewing the documentary. Includes a timeline on the reversal of the Earth's magnetic shield, simulated flip of the Earth's magnetic field, gallery of Aurora and information on how magnetic fields affect animal migration. If you choose to show the documentary to a class, there are ready-made handouts, questions and activities to accompany the viewing.
